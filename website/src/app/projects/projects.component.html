How researchers are using Morphic

Since its development in 2016, Morphic has allowed researchers to explore visible trait variation across species using online images. Projects leveraging Morphic span diverse taxa and research questions, including:

Color polymorphism and geographical variation

Studies on birds, insects, and mammals have used Morphic to quantify and compare color morphs across broad spatial scales.

Evolutionary ecology and behavior

Researchers have investigated how morph types relate to habitat use, diet, and interspecies interactions.

Conservation and range shifts

Some projects have used image data to track phenotypic changes associated with range expansion or environmental change.

Large-scale analysis

Morphic makes processing easier and more repeatable while removing duplicates, enabling cost-effective analysis of thousands of images.

Morphic has supported peer-reviewed research at institutions worldwide, with published studies in journals such as Ecology, The Condor, Journal of Avian Biology and Conservation Science and Practice. Our approach is especially valuable for cost-effective, large-scale analyses of visible traits using accessible online image data.
